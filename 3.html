The Hidden Helpers of Spring

When spring arrives, we see flowers blooming and trees growing. But there is a world we don’t see. It is a world of insects昆虫 working hard.

Job 1. Helping Plants Grow: One of the most important things insects do is pollinate受粉（する） . Insects like bees, butterflies, and mosquitoes carry pollen花粉 from one flower to another. This helps plants grow and make seeds. We need them for our apples, cherries, and peaches!

Job 2. Making Soil Healthy: Insects in the ground地面 help us, too! When ants bring food into the soil土 (dirt) and dig tunnelsトンネル , the soil is mixed and gets better for plants.

Job 3. Feeding Other Animals: Other animals eat insects, such as birds, frogs, and bats. Those beautiful bird songs in spring come from stomachs胃 full of bugs.

There are a lot of insects in the world! And they all do very different jobs. Some insects are very small, and some are very big. Some insects are very beautiful, and some are very ugly. Some insects are very useful, and some are very dangerous. But all insects are important for the world.

This is a dung beetle. It is a very important insect in the world. It eats animal poop, and it helps plants grow. It is very strong, too! It can pull 1,000 times its weight! That is like a person pulling a car!

This is a beetle. Actually, 40% of all insects are beetles! There are over 300,000 different species (kinds) of beetles. They are very strong, and they can live in many different places. Some beetles can even live in the ocean!

The special thing about beetles is their hard shell. This shell is made from the beetle's front wings. It covers their hind (back) wings and protects their bodies.

2 / 11

Ladybugs are very cute, and you can see black ladybugs with red spots and red ladybugs with black spots in Japan. They are one of the kinds of beetles in the world.

But ladybugs are not just cute! They are very useful, too! They eat aphids (plant bugs) and other small insects. So they help farmers grow plants. Farmers love ladybugs!

3 / 11

Rhinoceros beetles are very big and strong. They can lift over 100 times their weight! That is like a person lifting a car! They are some of the strongest animals on the Earth (for their size). They are very popular in Japan, and many people keep them as pets. The are the most famous bug (insect) in Japan.

Baby rhinoceros beetles are called larvae. They live in the ground and eat dirt, then they change into adults. Unfortunately, the adults don't live very long. They only live for about a year.

4 / 11

Stag beetles are another very famous bug in Japan. They are very strong, too, but they are smaller than rhinoceros beetles. They can lift 100 times their weight! That is like a person lifting a motorcycle! Stag beetles are very popular in Japan, and many people keep them as pets.

Stag beetles live longer than rhinoceros beetles. They can live for over a year and up to 3 years!

5 / 11

Mosquitoes are very small. They can bite you and suck your blood. And they are very dangerous because they can carry diseases like malaria and dengue fever. But don't worry too much. Mosquitoes are not very strong, and they are not very fast. A strong wind will blow them away, and you can catch them easily.

Mosquitoes actually help pollinate flowers like bees. But female (girl) mosquitoes need blood to make eggs, so they bite people. And mosquitoes are a very old animal, too. Even the dinosaurs were bitten by mosquitoes.

6 / 11

Ants are very small, but they are very strong. They can lift 50 times their weight! That is like a person lifting a car! Ants live in big groups called colonies. Some colonies have over 1 million ants! Ants are very smart, too. They can work together to find food and build homes. And they can even talk to each other with their antennae (feelers).

Did you know that most ants are females (girls)? Male (boy) ants only live for a few weeks. They are born to make eggs with the queen ant. After that, they die. The queen ant is the only one that can lay eggs. She can lay up to 1,000 eggs a day! That is like a person having 1,000 babies every day!

7 / 11

Dragonflies are really cool bugs. They are very fast and can fly up to 60 km/h! They can even fly backward! Dragonflies are very good hunters, too. They can catch other bugs in the air. And they have very big eyes. They can see almost all around them. But they can't see directly behind them.

Dragonflies live around water because they need it to lay eggs. Dragonfly babies, or nymphs, live in the water. They can other bugs, tadpoles (frog babies), and even small fish!

8 / 11

Butterflies are a really beautiful insect. And one of the most famous kinds of butterflies is the monarch butterfly in the USA. This butterfly flies all the way from Canada to Mexico in the fall, and then back in the spring! That is a long way, about 4,000 km!

One insect that looks like the butterfly is the moth. Many moths are brown and fly at night. Sometimes they go into houses because they like the light. How do you know if it is a moth or a butterfly? Moths usually rest with their wings open and down, and butterflies usually rest with their wings up.

9 / 11

Spiders are very useful animals because they eat many bugs. But they are not insects. They are arachnids. They have 8 legs, not 6 like insects. And they can make webs. Some spiders can even jump! And some spiders can even fly! They can use their webs to fly in the wind.

One close relative of spiders is the scorpion. They have 8 legs and a long tail with a stinger (poison needle) on the end.

10 / 11

Worms are not insects, either, but they are very useful. They live in the ground and eat dirt.

Actually, most worms are invasive species (not originally from that area). This can be bad for forests, but they are very good for farms. Farmers love worms because they help make the soil better for plants.
